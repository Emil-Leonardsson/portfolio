About me & my agile philosophy

↓ Next: Career

I'm a calm and structured agile leader with a background as tester, test lead, Scrum Master, Product Owner and Product Manager, all within complex, often regulated software environments. The common thread across every role is the same: create clarity where things are messy, and make sure teams have what they need to deliver with predictability and quality.

What Agile means to me: a mindset. Being ready for change and actively working toward continuous improvement that gives users and the business more value. Deliver often, with high quality, and validate that the value was actually delivered. Collaboration and dialogue come before tools.

What Scrum means to me: a framework that helps us embrace empiricism and self-organization, through clear roles, artifacts and events, but above all through empiricism's three pillars: transparency, inspection and adaptation. The values of Commitment, Focus, Openness, Respect and Courage aren't posters on the wall, they're how I actually work.

What Kanban means to me: seeing work as a flow that can be improved step by step. Through visualization, WIP‑limits and continuous measurement we create stability and focus. Kanban is easy to introduce because it's built on small adjustments to how we work, not major changes to roles or organization.

Coaching philosophy

I coach using GROW and CLEAR as frameworks and use Liberating Structures to get the whole team talking. My goal isn't to follow Scrum to the letter, but for the team to own its flow, dare to try new things and resolve impediments quickly. I measure what matters: cycle time, engagement and how effectively we actually remove blockers, so the coaching leads to shorter lead times, a more stable flow and a more self-sufficient team.

  • Open and transparent: I say what I think, respectfully, even when I disagree.
  • Works for continuous improvement, not a perfect process from day one.
  • Driven to deliver value. Ceremonies are a means, never the goal.
  • Thrives in complexity: multiple teams, many dependencies, high quality demands.
