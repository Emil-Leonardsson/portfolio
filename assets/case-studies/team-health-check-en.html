Team Health Check

A web-based survey app that lets teams anonymously check in on collaboration, pace and morale, and gives team admins a dashboard with trends over time. Built and specified entirely by me, from product requirements to data model to code.

My role: Product owner & builder (solo) Stack: React, TypeScript, Tailwind Backend: Bolt Database (Postgres, Auth, Edge Functions)

What the app does

Team members anonymously answer three short questions about collaboration, pace and morale via a unique team link, no login required. Team admins get a dashboard with average scores, a trend chart over time and the latest comments. A superadmin view controls a simple premium/free model and a global ad kill switch.

Anonymous data collection Trend visualization Role-based access (RLS) Multilingual (5 languages) Hybrid ad model

Why I built it

I wanted to see if I could take the same product thinking I use as a Product Owner, clear roles, deliberate scope boundaries, a documented data model and a prioritized backlog, and drive it myself from idea to working product.

Roles in the product

Three clearly scoped roles, a classic foundation for keeping permissions and flows simple.

RoleDescription
End userResponds via a unique team link. No login required, answers are fully anonymous, no user ID is linked to the response.
Team adminCreates an account, automatically gets a team linked to them, sees a dashboard with results for their own team and can share the team link.
SuperadminInternal view for setting premium status per team and controlling a global ad kill switch, access controlled via an allowlist table.

Core flows

  • 01
    Check-inThree questions at a time, 5-point scale, optional free-text comment. The submit button only activates once all three are answered.
  • 02
    Confirmation & trendAfter submitting, the participant sees a trend chart of the team's history, the same chart component as in the admin view, to give something back immediately.
  • 03
    Admin dashboardAverages per category, trend chart, the 8 latest comments, and an empty state with a clear call-to-action when no responses exist yet.
  • 04
    SuperadminGlobal ad toggle and per-team premium status, all locked behind an Edge Function with a service role.

Data model & security

EntityDescription
Teamid, name, slug (unique), admin_user_id, is_premium, created_at. A team is owned by one admin and has many check-ins.
Health Checkcollaboration, pace, morale (1–5), optional comment. No user ID is stored, fully anonymous.
SettingsGlobal setting (ads_enabled), one row, readable by everyone, writable only via service role.
SuperadminsAllowlist of email addresses controlling access to the superadmin view.

Row Level Security is enabled on all tables. A database trigger (guard_is_premium_update) prevents a team admin from setting their own team to premium, a deliberate safeguard against circumventing the business model.

How I prioritize

Just like in a real backlog, these are deliberate trade-offs, not things I forgot. Every constraint below was a decision about what was NOT the most important thing to build first.

Known constraints (deliberate trade-offs)

No payment logicPremium is set manually by the superadmin. No Stripe integration yet, not worth the complexity before the product was validated.
No limit on multiple check-ins/dayResponses are anonymous, so a limit would require cookie- or IP-based deduplication, a trade-off against simplicity I chose to skip early on.
Language choice isn't persistedResets on reload, low impact on the core experience.
No data exportNo CSV/PDF flow for reporting yet.

Next in the backlog

Payment integrationStripe for self-service premium upgrades.
Response rate limitingOne check-in per person per day, via cookie or one-time token in the team link.
Scheduled remindersLet admins schedule weekly reminders to the team.
Team aggregation for organizationsSupport for multiple teams under a shared organization view.
